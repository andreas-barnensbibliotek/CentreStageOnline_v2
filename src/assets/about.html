The Centre Stage Project

Centre Stage Online is part of the Centre Stage project which is a European collaborative project for professional women artists in the performing arts and music. It’s a project that aims to contribute to positive change at both individual and structural levels for women performing artists in Europe. The project started in September 2019 and lasts until 2022.

Centre Stage consists of the following three parts

Centre Stage | Core
A training and coaching programme for women performing artists to develop their communication and artistic leadership skills. 24 artists, eight each from Sweden, Ireland and Spain, will focus on self-promotion and career management skills in order to develop their careers transnationally.

Centre Stage | Programming for Diversity
In order to contribute to changes at a structural level, a Centre Stage film about equal opportunities in the performing arts is being produced. The film is intended to be used as a discussion material for meetings, seminars and the like in the performing arts industry throughout Europe.

At conferences and events in the three partner countries, discussions on gender equality, equal opportunities, and how to achieve change in the performing arts will also be arranged.

Centre Stage | Online
An online course for women performing artists around Europe who want to develop communication and artistic leadership skills. The course is open to performing artists and is aimed at the whole of Europe. The aim is that the participants after the course should have been given tools to develop a professional and successful artistic work.

Co-funded by the EU Creative Europe Programme

About the course

When creating this course we started in the conditions that many women in the performing arts encounter during their professional life and the course content is based on a gender perspective. But the course is of course open to all artistically active people in the performing arts, regardless of gender or gender identity.

To take the course you need to register. Your personal data is protected by GDPR (The General Data Protection Regulation), you do not commit to anything and the course is completely free of charge. The registration is simply for our follow-up of the project, in order for us to make sure that the right target group has been given access to the course content.

You can take the course at your own pace. The best way to do it is on your computer but it is also possible with tablet (we advise against using your mobile phone since you will be instructed to download worksheets).
